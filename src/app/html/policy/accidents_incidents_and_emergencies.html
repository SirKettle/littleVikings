The safety of your child is paramount and I will take every measure I can to protect your children from hurting themselves. However, sometimes accidents do happen. I have written the following procedure on how I will deal with such a situation:

  • I will ensure everyone is safe
  • I will comfort the child and reassure them.
  • I will assess the extent of their injuries and if necessary call for medical support/ambulance.
  • I will administer first aid where necessary. I have been trained to carry out to these procedures and have the required certificate.
  • I will call upon back up support by another registered childminder or known responsible adult to care for other children within my care, and where this is not possible I will take all the children with me or send the child alone with the ambulance team who will be safely trained and competent to care or the child.
  • Once the child is more settled I will contact you as soon as possible to inform you of the accident, and if necessary I will ask you to return to care for your child / meet me at the hospital.

After every accident, however minor I will:

  • Complete a report in my accident book.
  • Ask you to sign the report.

If the incident requires any medical treatment then I will:

  • Inform Ofsted
  • Inform my Insurance Company.
  • Contact the NCMA for additional advice/support.
  • Carry out a risk assessment to see if the accident is preventable in the future.

Incident:

  • If a child becomes unwell within my care I will monitor and record their symptoms such as:
    • Temperature
    • Sickness
    • Rash
  • I will administer medicine as required (with prior consent)
  • I will call you or your emergency contact to collect the child if they are too unwell to remain in my setting
  • Until you arrive the child will be kept as comfortable as possible under my supervision
  • The incident will be logged in the accident, incident and emergency log and will be signed by the parent as soon as is possible
  • oFsted will be notified within 14 days if two or more children are affected by food poisoning within my setting.

Emergency:

  • In the event of a fire, where the fire is small, I will use the fire blanket and/or fire extinguisher to put out the fire.
  • I will remove all the children from the building down the stairs and take them a safe distance from the building.
  • I will call the emergency services
  • In the event of an electrical fault, a flood or structural damage to the building, the children will be removed as safely and swiftly as possible and the emergency services called.
  • In the event of choking, asthma attack, anaphylactic shock etc, I will administer first aid as I am trained to do , call the emergency services and contact the parents.
  • All emergency events will be logged in the accident, incident and emergency log and will be signed by the parent as soon as possible.
